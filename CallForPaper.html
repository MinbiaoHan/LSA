Call for papers

This workshop is structured to encourage a lively exchange of ideas between members from these communities. We encourage all researchers working towards applying learning algorithms in strategic settings to submit at the workshop. Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Algorithms for High Dimensional Games
  • Coalitions, Coordination, Collective Action, and Cooperation
  • Computational Social Choice
  • Decision Making Under Uncertainty
  • Game Theoretical analysis of Multi-Agent Systems
  • Learning in Games (e.g., Equilibria Analysis)
  • Learning in Non-Stationary Environments
  • Mechanism Design
  • Multi-Agent Equilibrium Learning
  • Online Learning
  • Preference Elicitation
  • Robust Learning and Optimisation
  • Statistical Learning Theory
  • Uncertainty in AI

Submission Guidelines

We encourage submissions regarding ongoing or recent work related to the aformentioned topics. Papers that have appeared on arXiv. but haven't been accepted for publication are encouraged. Papers will be evaluated based on their relation to the themes of the workshop, quality, originality, and correctness. All submissions will be subject to a double-blind review process.

Submitted papers must not exceeed 8 pages, with any additional pages containing bilbiographic references only. Papers should be submitted in PDF document format and conform to the AAMAS 2022 formatting guidelines. Accepted papers will be listed on the workshop website, and authors will be invited to present a poster at a virtual poster session. Additionally, a select number of submissions will be invited to give a short oral presentation. Two accepted papers will be nominated to appear in Springer special issues, consisting of best workshop papers from AAMAS 2022 and visionary papers repsectively.
